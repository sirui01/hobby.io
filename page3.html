Diet

When you go into a pet shop, there are a wide variety of food available for different types of cats! Generally, cats need a balance of both dry and wet food. For dry food, I personally recommend "Royal Canin". They have a wide variety of dry food, with specialised formulas targeted for different cats. For instance, they have targeted dry foods for cats suffering with diabetes, liver disease, or those who are sensitive to their food.

Exercise

It's a well-known fact that cats are generally way less active than dogs. Generally, most cats do not require regular walks and strolls. Instead, they can spend their whole day sleeping and only waking up for their meals (at least that is what my cats do). Hence, it is important to watch their diet and ensure that they are still getting their exercise at home to prevent them from becoming overweight. An overweight cat may pose an increased risk to certain health diseases, such as diabetes or heart diseases.
To factor in some exercise with your cats, make sure to play with them frequently! Equip yourself with a bunch of cat teasers or other kinds of cat toys and start engaging your cat! Here's a short video on how Rummie plays with his favourite cat teaser toy.
